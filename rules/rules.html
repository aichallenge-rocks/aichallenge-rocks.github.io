Sponsor

The presenting sponsor of this Open AI Challenge is EPAM Systems Company.

No purchase, payment, or donation of any kind is necessary to enter or win.

Participant Categories

The competition is EPAM Brest Open AI Challenge. It means that location is Brest, Belarus and we are welcome engineers and students from Brest and Brest region to take part. People from other locations can participate too but organizers will not send prizes to other locations.

There are two categories of participants: employees of EPAM Systems Brest branch and external people (engineers and students). All participants play in one tournament, each category has 1st, 2nd and 3rd places and prizes for them.

AI Challenge Objective

Each player will need to implement an intelligent bot that plays a Khet game against all the other players. It is a unique challenge that combines battle strategy, software engineering and artificial intelligence. You can find the details of Khet game rules here.

Common Rules

The Open AI Challenge is a round-robin competition among software developers or students. A round-robin tournament means that each bot meets all other bots in turn. Each pair of bots plays two games: each bot play first hand at one game and second hand at another.

The Open AI Challenge starts at April 1, 2015, and ends at April 30, 2015.

Scoring

Once bot wins the game player gets score of 1. Lost bot gets score of 0. In case of draw both bots get score of 0.5. Once bot hangs or throws an error during the game then it evaluates as loss.

The winners are competitors with the highest aggregate scores earned in all rounds in each category. The current results are available at scoreboard.

Warning

Players who are deemed to violate the spirit of fair and sportsmanlike competition will be disqualified from all (future) competitions without any opportunity for appeal. In particular, memory scanning, intentionally losing games, and behaviour conditional on the opponent's identity are prohibited.

Any attempt to disrupt the normal operation of the contest software or the contest servers will result in the immediate involvement of law enforcement officials and disqualification from all (future) competitions.
